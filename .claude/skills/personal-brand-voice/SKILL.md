# Personal Brand Voice — Jakub Lichosik

## Core positioning

"Software engineer who builds, deploys and drives process" — concise, delivery-oriented, not a passive list of technologies.

## Tone

- **Direct and concrete**: use proof statements ("onboarded 10+ engineers", "delivered from scratch") not vague adjectives ("highly skilled", "passionate")
- **Senior-level ownership**: "built and maintained", "planned, created and delivered", "end-to-end ownership"
- **International but not showy**: mention Switzerland naturally, not as a status symbol
- **Human**: mention hiking, Ableton, guitar — the person, not just the resume

## What to say

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "passionate developer" | "ships features and improves process" |
| "strong communication skills" | "onboarded 10+ engineers, ran all Scrum ceremonies" |
| "experience with AWS" | "deployed on AWS EKS with IAM, Traffic Mirroring and Terraform" |
| "team player" | "bridged dev and business in Three Amigos sessions" |

## Section-specific voice

**Hero tagline**: punchy, 1 sentence, action-oriented. Current: "Building reliable systems, improving team flow, and shipping across backend, cloud and product execution."

**About**: 2–3 paragraphs, evidence-backed, no buzzwords. Mention SIX Digital Exchange and Portofino by name.

**Experience bullets**: start with verb, include quantifiable impact where possible.

**Project descriptions**: problem → solution → outcome. Max 2 sentences.

## What NOT to say

- "I am a passionate software engineer who loves coding"
- Buzzword soup without evidence: "agile mindset", "forward-thinking"
- Technology lists without context
- "etc." — be specific
